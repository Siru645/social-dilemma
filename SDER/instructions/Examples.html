Three examples illustrate how the tokens moved to the Group Account are related to the values of the Individual Account and Group Account. For each example, we show how the value of the Individual and Group Account change in a corresponding table.

  • Example A: If a person subtracts 5 tokens from the Group Account, that increases the value of their own Individual Account by 5 ECUs and reduces the value of the Group Account by 5 × 3 = 15 ECUs.
  • Example B: If a person adds 0 tokens to the Group Account, that reduces the value of their Individual Account by 0 ECU and adds 0 × 3 = 0 ECUs to the value of the Group Account.
  • Example C: If a person adds 5 tokens to the Group Account, that reduces the value of their Individual Account by 5 ECUs and adds 5 × 3 = 15 ECUs to the value of the Group Account.
Example Decision Change in Value (ECUs)
Tokens Moved Individual Account Group Account
A 0 0 0
B 5 -5 +15
C 10 -10 +30

After you make your decisions, you will be informed of the starting group account, which of your decisions is played, the value of your Individual Account and the Group Account, and your final earnings. You will be paid in U.S. dollars using a conversion rate for $1 for every {{exchange_rate}} ECU{{if exchange_rate != 1}} 's {{endif}} of earnings from the experiment. Your total earnings will be the final value of tokens (in ECUs) in your own Individual Account from both your selected decision and one-fourth of the final value of tokens in the Group Account (in ECUs).
